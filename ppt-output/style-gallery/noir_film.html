Urban Light
after Midnight

The 90 minutes between 02:00 and 03:30 reveal a city no daytime survey captures. Street luminance drops to 12 % of its peak; noise floors collapse by 24 dB. The city does not sleep — it shifts registers, exposing the load-bearing structures usually hidden beneath commerce and motion.

"The hour the city stops performing itself is the only hour you can photograph what it actually is."

Across 6 boroughs and 188 locations, this study logged ambient light, pedestrian density, acoustic signature, and infrastructure utilisation at 15-minute intervals over 14 consecutive nights. The data challenges the assumption that after-hours cities are merely quieter versions of their daytime selves.
